Who We Are

ABOUT
IDEA FOUNDRY

The Idea Foundry is a student-led think tank focused on helping the Bumbogo community tackle the effects of climate change through sustainable agriculture. We research local challenges and design practical, climate-smart solutions that strengthen resilience, improve livelihoods, and create lasting impact.

Through collaboration and innovation, we turn ideas into action, working toward a future where communities can adapt, thrive, and sustain their environment.

The Challenge

PROBLEM
STATEMENT

Bumbogo's industrial environment and degraded ecosystem threaten the food security and livelihoods of thousands of families who depend on agriculture.

01

Industrial Degradation

Heavy industrial activity has stripped topsoil of its nutrients and biological diversity, severely limiting crop viability for local farmers.

02

Declining Harvests

Families are experiencing dramatically reduced yields year after year, pushing many into food insecurity and financial strain.

03

Climate Unpredictability

Erratic rainfall, rising temperatures, and extreme weather events make traditional farming calendars unreliable and risky.

Why We Exist

"TO EMPOWER COMMUNITIES IN BUMBOGO BY PROMOTING SUSTAINABLE AGRICULTURE THAT RESTORES SOIL HEALTH, IMPROVES HARVESTS, AND REDUCES THE IMPACT OF CLIMATE CHANGE."

Our Answer

OUR
SOLUTION

Our solution promotes sustainable agriculture through tree planting to improve soil and reduce erosion, climate-smart irrigation for reliable farming, kitchen gardens for better nutrition, and recycling initiatives that turn waste into income — creating both environmental and economic benefits.

Ecology

Tree Planting & Land Restoration

Improve soil fertility and reduce erosion across Bumbogo.

Nutrition

Sustainable Kitchen Gardens

Year-round gardens providing consistent, nutritious harvests for families.

Water

Climate-Smart Irrigation

Reliable farming water access even during dry seasons.

Economy

Recycled Art Initiative

Turn waste into income-generating creative products for the community.

How We Got Here

OUR eLAB
JOURNEY

Four focused phases that took us from a classroom hypothesis to a real community solution in Bumbogo, Rwanda.

01

Research

We studied the intersection of climate change and agricultural productivity across Rwanda's communities.

02

Region Selection

Bumbogo emerged as the most critically affected industrial zone — where our intervention could create real change.

03

Community Listening

We mapped real agricultural patterns and documented first-hand climate impact stories from residents.

04

Solution Design

Armed with community insight, we engineered a practical kitchen garden system tailored to Bumbogo's constraints.
